Who We Are

We are dedicated to contribute to the promotion, protection and defense of all civil, political, economic, social and cultural rights as set out in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights of 1948, the international pact regarding civil and political rights, the Geneva Agreement of 1949 and the United Nations Conventions and their subsequent charges.

It strives to promote democracy and the consolidation of the Rule of Law, a just international social and economic order and sustainable development which respect the environment.

In addition, SCHRPD works to strengthen the capacities of civil society, private sector and government to work together for responsive democratic governance, social and economic development. SCHRPD programs provide training, technical assistance and grants designed to strengthen democratic values, institutions and processes and promote democratic governance, economic and social development. .

Our Vision

To become a leading independent human rights and development organization that promotes, and defends human rights and supports civil society, private sector and government to work together for responsive democratic governance, social and economic development through strengthening of their capacities around the world.

Our Mission

To effectively support the implementation of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights of 1948, the international pact regarding civil, political, economic, social and cultural rights, the Geneva Agreement of 1949 and the United Nations Conventions and their subsequent changes and reinforce international human rights law.

Background

The Sanyuworld Centre for Human Rights, Peace and Development (SCHRPD) is an independent human rights, peace and development non – governmental organization (NGO) that was established in the early 2021 in Kampala, Uganda. The organization is dedicated to contribute to the promotion, protection and defense of all civil, political, economic, social and cultural rights as set out in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights of 1948, the international pact regarding civil and political rights, the Geneva Agreement of 1949 and the United Nations Conventions and their subsequent changes.

Our History

SCHRPD started with a group of three individuals as founders seeking to leverage a unique spirit of social justice to promote and protect human rights, peace and development worldwide. Since then, our innovative programming has changed the lives of refugees and immigrants, women, ethnic and religious minorities, children, and other marginalized communities. We investigate and expose human rights violations, represent immigrants and refugees seeking asylum, train and assist groups that protect human rights, engage the public, policy-makers, and children; and push for legal reform and advocate for sound policy. The SCHRPD is an independent, non-partisan, non-governmental international human rights organization.

Our Approach

The Sanyuworld Centre for Human Rights, Peace and Development (SCHRPD) believes that each one of us has a part to play in defending human rights. By engaging volunteers in hands-on human rights work, The SCHRPD not only accomplishes critical research and advocacy, we transform volunteers into advocates for human rights.

The SCHRPD works on two levels: the systemic level and the individual direct services level. The Advocates' unique approach grounds policy work in the lived experiences of our partners and clients. When our clients experience injustice, we help them and advocate to change the systems that perpetuate those injustices. When our partners report barriers to safety and security, we identify the gaps in legal protections and develop solutions.
